​How the Simpsons Was Written​

When John Swartzwelder was working on The Simpsons, he used to “create an imperfect world and then improve it.”

Though first drafts were hard for him to write, rewriting was comparatively easy. Thus, he wrote his scripts all the way through as fast as possible. By doing that, he says: “I’ve taken a very hard job, writing, and turned it into an easy one, rewriting, overnight.”

Andre Agassi

I collect stories about the insane length some people go to perform at the highest level. Over the weekend, I added a new one to the bank about Andre Agassi, the former world #1 tennis player.

He lost his first three matches against Boris Becker. Frustrated, he started watching the film when he realized that he could predict the direction of the serve by the movement of Becker’s tongue.

Agassi said: “I’m not kidding. He would go into his rocking motion – his same routine – and just as he was about to toss the ball, he would stick his tongue out. And it would either be right in the middle of his lip, or it’d be to the left corner of his lip.”

The hardest part, he said, wasn’t predicting where the serve would go. It was playing so that Becker wouldn’t know Agassi’s secret.

Years later, Agassi told Becker about what he learned. When Becker heard the news, he laughed and said: ‘I used to go home all the time and just tell my wife, ‘It’s like he reads my mind'… Little did I know, you were just reading my tongue.”

​Dragon Blood Trees​

Photo of the Week

This is a photo of my high school. Someday, I'll talk more about how every single student used to gather into a single room to meditate every morning. But for now, I want to talk about a high school class I'd like to teach someday.

Every kid would get $1,000 to start their own business. I'd like students to succeed, but it doesn't really matter. The point is that everybody should know what it feels like to sell something to the free market.

Selling something teaches you stuff that you can only understand experientially. The cheaper it becomes to start a business, the further that $1,000 can go. Success is nice, but because it'll inspire curiosity and motivation, failure is kind of the point here.
